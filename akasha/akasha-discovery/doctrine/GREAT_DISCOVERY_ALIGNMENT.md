# The Great Discovery — Akasha Alignment

## Status: candidate for constellation admission

This document maps the Great Discovery engine's concepts
onto Akasha's structural vocabulary, so that the two systems
can interoperate without either having to abandon its own
design integrity.

---

## What the Great Discovery Is, in Akasha Terms

The Great Discovery is a **knowledge graph pressure engine**.

It grows a graph, measures structural pressure (compression ratio,
entropy, hole count), detects forbidden motifs (configurations that
destabilize the topology), and watches holes sharpen as the surrounding
structure fills.

In Akasha terms:

| Great Discovery Concept | Akasha Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Node | World primitive (entity, state, process...) |
| Edge | Relation class (transitions_to, constrained_by...) |
| Motif | Structural pattern (recurrent subgraph) |
| Forbidden motif | Constraint violation boundary |
| Hole | Missing candidate — structural debt |
| Compression spike | Entropy accumulation event (RCF Law 4) |
| Governance layer | akasha-guardian (alignment enforcement) |
| Convergence state | Attractor reached / phase transition |
| Named hole | Discovery candidate |
| Question node | akasha-discovery gap hypothesis |

## How the Great Discovery Feeds akasha-discovery

The Great Discovery produces **named holes** — structural positions
in the knowledge graph that the surrounding topology requires
but that have not yet been filled.

These named holes are precisely what akasha-discovery's candidate
format is designed to hold.

The pipeline:

```
Great Discovery engine
        ↓
named hole (structural debt)
        ↓
translated to akasha-discovery candidate format
  - title: <hole name or description>
  - domain: <which domain graph it appears in>
  - observation: <the structural pattern that shaped the hole>
  - hypothesis: <what the hole implies must exist>
  - supporting_evidence: <forbidden motifs that define the hole's edges>
  - confidence: derived from hole sharpness + lens convergence
  - trajectory: full derivation from initial graph state
        ↓
human steward review
        ↓
accepted → akasha-world primitive or relation
rejected → scar recorded (shapes future discovery)
```

## The Pressure Field in RCF Terms

The Great Discovery's three-force pressure field:
- hole attraction (pull toward unfilled structural positions)
- void tension (pull toward less-explored regions)
- forbidden repulsion (push away from constraint-violating configurations)

In RCF terms:
- hole attraction = entropy accumulation around high-cost transitions
- void tension = low-entropy regions (many novel patterns, open exploration)
- forbidden repulsion = constraint backreaction (approaching fracture limit)

The Great Discovery already implements the RCF calculus.
It just had different names for it.

## What the Great Discovery Needs from Akasha

The Great Discovery currently operates on structural topology alone —
nodes and edges without semantic content in the early phases.

What Akasha provides:

1. **A domain vocabulary** — akasha-world's primitives and relations
   give the Great Discovery's nodes names and types. This is Phase 2
   of the Great Discovery roadmap ("meaning introduced without forcing it").

2. **A lens layer** — akasha-lens provides analytical frameworks that
   can examine the Great Discovery's graph from multiple perspectives,
   increasing confidence in named holes and detecting boundary conditions.

3. **A scar ledger** — akasha-discovery's scar doctrine gives the
   Great Discovery a way to record not just what holes were found
   but what happened when they were filled (or failed to be filled).

4. **Human stewardship** — Akasha's governance structure provides
   the human-in-the-loop that the Great Discovery needs for its
   "named holes surfaced to external consumers" open item (Phase 4).

---

## What Akasha Needs from the Great Discovery

The Great Discovery has already solved several problems that Akasha
has only described philosophically:

1. **The pressure dynamics are real** — compression spikes as boundary
   detection, hole sharpening as discovery convergence. Akasha's
   discovery doctrine describes these in words; the Great Discovery
   implements them in code.

2. **The recursion is proven** — the engine asking questions about
   its own question-asking (depth=1 meta-questions) is Axiom 8
   (Iteration) running. The map sharpening the mapmaker is real.

3. **Convergence state tracking** — the five stability states
   (STABLE, DEADLOCKED, EXPANDING, DIVERGENT, OSCILLATORY) are
   exactly the kind of structured failure semantics that Akasha's
   RCF substrate describes but has not yet implemented for its
   discovery engines.

## Alignment Statement

The Great Discovery aligns with:

- **Axiom 1 (Coherence)** — forbidden motifs are the engine's primary signal.
  Contradiction is diagnostic, not fatal.
- **Axiom 2 (Discoverability)** — holes are structural signals.
  Gaps demand, they do not merely suggest.
- **Axiom 5 (Traceability)** — full trajectory from initial graph state
  to named hole. Nothing appears without a derivation.
- **Axiom 8 (Iteration)** — the engine revises itself. Recursion to
  depth=1 (meta-questions). The map sharpens the mapmaker.
- **Axiom 9 (Stewardship)** — named holes are surfaced to human consumers.
  The engine proposes. Stewards decide.
- **Axiom 10 (Continuity)** — the graph accumulates. Forbidden motifs
  accumulate. The scar ledger accumulates. Progress compounds.
